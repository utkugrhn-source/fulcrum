## 1. The brand

Fulcrum is an open daily orthopaedic literature digest — pulls fresh PubMed articles every morning and ranks them by journal tier, study type and recency, with a weekly editor's pick on the front page. Built by an academic orthopaedic surgeon at Kyrenia University (Cyprus). International audience: orthopaedic surgeons, residents, sports-medicine clinicians, academic readers.

Voice = three layers blended:
- **Academic discipline** — like a medical journal masthead.
- **Varsity locker-room** — college athletics confidence, brass / navy / cream, jersey numerals.
- **Editorial magazine** — italic display serif, generous whitespace, folio numbering.

Personality: disciplined, scholarly, slightly maximalist in typography, restrained in geometry. Not cartoonish, not Silicon Valley, not "wellness".

## 2. The mark — concept

The mark fuses two icons:

### (a) Andry's sapling — orthopaedic heritage
The traditional symbol of orthopaedics since 1741, from Nicolas Andry's *L'Orthopédie*. A young crooked tree tied to a straight wooden pole with cords — visualising the literal Greek meaning of orthopaedics, *orthos* + *paidios*, "straight child / straight growth". Used in some form by AAOS (American Academy of Orthopaedic Surgeons), the British Orthopaedic Association, the EFORT congress, and most orthopaedic societies worldwide. **This element is non-negotiable visual heritage.** The mark must read instantly as orthopaedic to anyone in the field.

### (b) The fulcrum — brand differentiator
The brand is named **Fulcrum** — Latin for the pivot point of a lever. Biomechanics is built on levers; surgery itself is leverage. The straight support pole in Andry's classic drawing already implies a fulcrum — we want to make that **explicit**. A small triangular pivot at the very base of the support pole. The pole rests on the pivot. Lever + sapling + correction = one continuous concept.

The fulcrum detail supplies the brand-specific differentiation without disturbing the orthopaedic reading.

## 3. Visual references

Study these first:
- Nicolas Andry's original 1741 illustration ("L'Orthopédie ou l'art de prévenir et de corriger dans les enfants, les difformités du corps")
- AAOS official mark (modern abstracted version of the same sapling)
- British Orthopaedic Association seal
- Princeton Tigers / Yale Bulldogs varsity crests — composition discipline, restraint
- Vesalius anatomy engravings — line quality, gravity, depth
- The Lancet masthead
- Editorial logotypes: *The Athletic* wordmark, *The Economist* red, *New Left Review* mark

## 4. Avoid

- Heraldic shields ("crest" feel — too aggressive for a journal product)
- Snakes / caduceus / rod of Asclepius (overused; we want the orthopaedic-specific symbol, not generic medical)
- Generic medical crosses
- Bone illustrations or femur silhouettes (cliché)
- Skeletal X-ray motifs
- Tech-startup geometry (clean circles, gradients, swooshes)
- Skeuomorphic woodgrain, rope braid textures, or shaded foliage — keep it line-based
- Cute / mascot interpretations

## 5. Style direction

- **Engraved line art** — confident strokes, like a 19th-century anatomy atlas reproduced for modern web
- **Geometric clarity** — modernisation comes from clean proportions, not flat-design abstraction
- **Optically balanced** — the sapling leans; the overall composition must still feel centred at 16px favicon scale
- **Two-color minimum / four-color maximum** — see palette below
- The fulcrum pivot at the base should be small and intentional — it reads as a foundation, not as a separate element competing with the sapling

## 6. Color palette — "Varsity Codex"

| Role | Color | Hex |
|---|---|---|
| Primary structure (pole, fulcrum pivot, groundline) | Brass | `#B89968` |
| Sapling (organic curve, leaves) | Navy ink | `#0F2540` |
| Rope ties (accent only) | Blood red | `#9E2A2B` |
| Paper / surface | Cream | `#F4ECDB` |

Constraints:
- Must work on cream `#F4ECDB` AND on navy `#0F2540` without modification
- Must work in single-colour: navy on cream, cream on navy
- Must hold up as a 16px favicon — drop the rope ties at favicon scale if necessary

## 7. Composition rules (current working sketch — refine, don't blindly follow)

- Vertical bounding box 1 : 1.1 (slightly taller than wide), allowing the sapling to lean
- Pole = dead vertical, brass, 2.5–3 stroke units
- Sapling = visual hero — navy organic S-curve, 1.5× the pole's stroke, leaning right
- Three or four rope ties at uniform spacing, blood red, thin
- Three small leaf shapes at the sapling crown, navy, organic (not perfect circles)
- Fulcrum pivot = small isoceles triangle at base of pole, brass, 4–5 units wide, on a thin brass groundline
- 4-unit padding on all sides from the bounding box

## 8. Deliverables

1. **Primary mark** — full colour, SVG + AI source

4. **Monochrome cream** (for navy backgrounds)

   - Horizontal: mark left, wordmark right

6. **Editor's seal variant** — circular framed version with the mark inside concentric red rings (`#9E2A2B`) and "EDİTÖR · SEÇİM" set in JetBrains Mono around the perimeter (~80px target use)
7. **Construction grid** — one diagram showing proportions, clear space, optical centre

## 9. Typography to pair with

Wordmark = **Source Serif Pro Italic 700** for "Fulcrum", with the initial "F" in blood red `#9E2A2B` and the rest in navy `#0F2540`. Supporting monospace = **JetBrains Mono** for editorial micro-meta (folio numbers, week numbers, seal text).
